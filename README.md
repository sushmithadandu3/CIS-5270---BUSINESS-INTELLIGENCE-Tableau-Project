# CIS-5270---BUSINESS-INTELLIGENCE-Tableau-Project

**Project Overview: H1B Non-Immigrant Labor Visa Data Analysis on Tableau**

**Introduction**

The project delves into the analysis of H-1B non-immigrant labor visa petitions using Tableau. 
The H-1B visa is crucial for temporary foreign workers in the U.S., often sought by international students post-higher education. 
The project aims to unravel insights into the visa application process, and its impact on the American job market, wages, and the economy.

**Motivation**

Driven by the curiosity of international students, the project seeks to understand trends and patterns in the H-1B visa system. 

The motivation stems from the need to grasp the program's usage, its impact on the job market, and potential areas for improvement.

**Tableau**

Tableau is employed for visualizing and interpreting the H-1B visa dataset. 

The tool aids in creating insightful visualizations and dashboards to facilitate a comprehensive understanding of the data.

**Data Description**

The dataset, obtained from Kaggle, spans 2011-2017, including details like employer information, job titles, locations, wages, and petition statuses. 

Key fields include CASE_SUBMITTED, DECISION_DATE, EMP_NAME, SOC_NAME, FULL_TIME_POSITION, PW_UNIT, WAGE, H-1B_DEPENDENT, WILLFUL_VIOLATOR, and more.

**Data Cleaning**

Cleaning steps include removing duplicates, handling null values, changing currency formats, eliminating extra spaces, and splitting columns. 

These ensure data accuracy and consistency, enhancing the reliability of subsequent analyses.

**Data Visualizations**

-Top 10 States with Highest H1B Approvals: Geographical map with rankings showcasing states with the most H1B approvals.

-Prevailing Wage vs. Actual Wage Over Years: Dual-axis graph illustrating the relationship between prevailing and actual wages from 2009-2017.

-Median Salary Differences Across States: Box and whisker plot highlighting variations in median salaries for different job roles and states.

-Forecast of 2018's H1B Applications: Trend lines predicting H1B certified applications for 2018.

-Average Number of H1B Applications (2015-2017): Dates and Reference Line graph depicting the average number of applications submitted yearly.

**Dashboards**

A comprehensive dashboard includes visualizations answering specific analysis questions, offering a holistic view of H-1B visa trends.

**Storytelling**

The project narrative outlines the journey, from data collection to insights.

It provides a coherent narrative around geographical trends, wage correlations, and forecasts, aligning with external articles on H-1B visa analysis.

**Conclusion**

The project contributes valuable insights for policymakers, employers, and researchers, aiding informed decision-making regarding the H-1B visa program and its impact on the U.S. labor market.
